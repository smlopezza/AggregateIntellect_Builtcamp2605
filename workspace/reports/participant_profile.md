## project_idea

### concept
A bilingual (English/Spanish) AI companion agent for Latino newcomers to Canada — focused on helping them understand *why* networking matters and actually enjoy it, starting with coffee chats. The agent also helps newcomers set their career goals and tracks progress toward them in a way that feels encouraging and celebratory. The core transformation: from fear and overwhelm to confidence, connection, and a sense of momentum.

### problem_space
Newcomers to Canada (less than 5 years in the country) who haven't yet found work in their profession. Many face survival job pressure, immigration stress, and cultural unfamiliarity with Canadian professional norms like coffee chats and informal networking. The target users are Latino newcomers, with the architecture designed to expand to other demographics in the future.

### proposed_approach
Tech stack TBD — open to learning something new. Bilingual support (English + Spanish) is a core requirement. Starting with Latino newcomers as the initial demographic, designed to scale to others. Key design constraints: does NOT replace a mentor or career coach, does NOT answer immigration questions (redirects to government resources), does NOT replace mental health support (routes to appropriate resources when concerns are detected).

### success_criteria
- Newcomers go from dreading coffee chats to genuinely enjoying them
- Users can track and celebrate their networking progress
- Real users sharing their experience publicly (LinkedIn)
- Bilingual experience that feels natural, not translated
- At least 3 active users by Sprint 3
